Honey Trehan’s Satluj (previously titled Panjab 95) was supposed to have its world premiere at the Toronto International Film Festival 2023. It’s been locked into a battle with the Censor board. The film got a brief release on Zee5 (which doesn’t require a CBFC certificate) on July 3. Two days later, on July 5, the film was pulled down. Zee5 announced this on multiple platforms through a statement. Trehan has said he has full faith in the platform, and he’s praying the film will be back soon. Late into the film, a ghost enters a room. It speaks to a man, a state witness who turned hostile in the scene before. The ghost recounts the harrowing circumstances of its death. The eeriness of the scene reminded me of a film by Gurvinder/Anup Singh. A grounded police procedural about a missing human rights activist till then, Trehan’s film boldly cuts across into surreal territory. And yet, it’s his emotional honesty that ensures the wild swerve doesn’t feel jarring.
