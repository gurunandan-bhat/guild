It is amusing to observe that Bollywood filmmakers seem to inhabit their own male multiverses. In Yash Chopra’s world, men serenade women with flowers and sweet nothings. For Sandeep Reddy Vanga’s men, romance is best expressed through anger issues and abuse. Imtiaz Ali populates his stories with man-children who require an older person to explain the meaning of love. Sooraj Barjatya’s men barely speak, whispering as though struggling to swallow the sanskari pill he personally concocted for them. And then there is Luv Ranjan, whose men are perpetual sufferers and whose women exist solely to ruin their peace.
